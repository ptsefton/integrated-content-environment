Glassware for inert atmosphere reactions was pre-dried in an oven (minimum temperature 180 <sup>o</sup>C) and cooled to room temperature under vacuum before introducing argon.  </p><p>Water used in work-ups was de-ionised and all aqueous solutions were saturated unless otherwise stated.  Petrol refers to the fraction of light petroleum ether with boiling point 40 <span class="spCh spChx2013">&#x2013;</span> 60 <sup>o</sup>C.  This and all other solvents used in chromatography and work-ups was pre-distilled.  Dichloromethane, methanol, acetonitrile and toluene used in reaction mixtures were distilled from calcium hydride.  Diethyl ether and tetrahydrofuran (along with a triphenylmethane indicator) were distilled from calcium hydride and lithium aluminium hydride.  Anhydrous benzene and <i>N,N</i><span class="spCh spChx2011">&#x2011;</span>dimethylformamide were used as supplied commercially.  Triethylamine, <i>N,N</i><span class="spCh spChx2011">&#x2011;</span>diisopropylethylamine and diisopropylamine were all distilled from calcium hydride and stored over potassium hydroxide.   All other reagents and solvents were purified by standard procedures or were used as obtained from commercial sources as appropriate.</p><p>Flash chromatography was performed over silica using Breckland Scientific Supplies Silica Gel 60 (230 <span class="spCh spChx2013">&#x2013;</span> 400 mesh) or alumina using Merck Aluminium Oxide 90 (standardised).  Compressed air was used to elute the column. Analytical thin layer chromatography was carried out on pre-coated glass-backed plates (Merck Kieselgel 60 F<sub>254</sub>) and visualised by UV fluorescence (<span class="spCh spChx3bb">&#x3BB;</span> = 254 nm) and staining by acidic ammonium molybdate (IV) with heating.  Where this was not suitable use of alternative staining agents, <i>e.g. </i>acidic ethanolic vanillin or basic aqueous potassium permanganate (VII), are noted in the text.</p><p>All data was collected at the Department of Chemistry, University of Cambridge.  Melting points were measured on a Reichert hot stage apparatus on borosilicate glass plates (BDH, thickness number 1).  Optical rotations were measured using either a Perkin-Elmer Model 343 or Model 241 polarimeter with a sodium lamp and are reported as [<span class="spCh spChx3b1">&#x3B1;</span>]<sup>X</sup><i style="font-variant:small-caps; "><sub><span>D</span></sub></i> (<i>c</i>, CHCl<sub>3</sub>) where X is the temperature in degrees centigrade and <i>c </i>is the concentration in grams / 100 mL CHCl<sub>3</sub>.  Infra-red spectra were recorded either neat or as thin films from evaporation of a chloroform or diethyl ether solution on a Perkin-Elmer Spectrum One FT-IR, fitted with a Universal ATR Sampling Accessory.  Absorbances (<span class="spCh spChx3bd">&#x3BD;</span><sub>max </sub>/ cm<sup>-1</sup>) are reported as s = strong, m= medium, w = weak, br&#xA0;=&#xA0;broad.  Proton magnetic resonance (<sup>1</sup>H NMR) spectra were recorded at 400 MHz, 500 MHz and 600 MHz on a Bruker Biospin Avance 400, 500 (cryoprobe) and 600 machine respectively. Measurements were taken at room temperature in either CDCl<sub>3</sub> or C<sub>6</sub>D<sub>6</sub> and are reported in the format: chemical shift <span class="spCh spChx3b4">&#x3B4;</span> / ppm (number of protons, multiplicity, coupling constant <i>J </i>/ Hz, assignment).  Coupling constants are quoted to the nearest 0.1 Hz with s = singlet, d = doublet, t = triplet, q = quartet, m = multiplet and br = broad signal.  The residual protic solvent CHCl<sub>3</sub> (<span class="spCh spChx3b4">&#x3B4;</span><sub>H</sub> = 7.26 ppm) or C<sub>6</sub>H<sub>6</sub> (<span class="spCh spChx3b4">&#x3B4;</span><sub>H&#xA0;</sub>=&#xA0;7.15 ppm) was used as an internal reference.  Carbon magnetic resonance (<sup>13</sup>C&#xA0;NMR) spectra were recorded on the same machines in either CDCl<sub>3</sub> or C<sub>6</sub>D<sub>6 </sub>at room temperature and the residual protic solvent CHCl<sub>3</sub> (<span class="spCh spChx3b4">&#x3B4;</span><sub>C</sub> = 77 ppm, triplet) or C<sub>6</sub>H<sub>6</sub> (<span class="spCh spChx3b4">&#x3B4;</span><sub>C</sub> = 128.6 ppm) was used as the internal reference.  The spectra were assigned as fully as possible using a variety of 2-D and nOe techniques.  High resolution mass spectra were obtained on a Waters LCT Premier spectrometer with Micromass MS software using electrospray ionisation (+ESI).</p><h1><a id="id2" name="id2"><!--id2--></a>A1.2 Experimental Data for Section 2.1</h1><p class="exptl-compound"><a href="#03ae2e47d4c4424aa06128c4b67aee93" name="Object1" class="lightbox" title=""><img alt="Object1" class="fr1" height="135" src="appendix_example_files/3c3e0307_119x135bw.png" style="border:0px; vertical-align: top" width="119" title=""/><span class="lightbox-link">     <!-- --></span><div id="03ae2e47d4c4424aa06128c4b67aee93"><img width="500" alt="mol9.png" src="../../packages/demonstration/media/compound/mol9.png" height="500" title=""/><p class="lightbox-caption"></p></div></a></p><p class="center" style="keep-with-next:always; ">(+/-)</p><p class="comnum">       69</p><p class="compound-name" style="keep-together:always; "><a href="#0819d80543b44773bdca6b0d5f2bf95f" class="lightbox" title=""><span class="Internet_20_link"><i>rac-</i></span><span class="Internet_20_link">(1</span><span class="Internet_20_link"><i>S</i></span><span class="Internet_20_link"><i><sup>*</sup></i></span><span class="Internet_20_link">,8a</span><span class="Internet_20_link"><i>S</i></span><span class="Internet_20_link"><i><sup>*</sup></i></span><span class="Internet_20_link">)-1,3-dimethyl-4,5,6,7,8,8a-hexahydro-2(1</span><span class="Internet_20_link"><i>H</i></span><span class="Internet_20_link">)-azulenone</span><span class="lightbox-link">     <!-- --></span><div class="embedded-html" title="" id="0819d80543b44773bdca6b0d5f2bf95f"><script src="../../jquery.js" type="text/javascript"><!-- --></script><script src="../../ice.js" type="text/javascript"><!-- --></script><script src="../../skin/jquery-event-drag.js" type="text/javascript"><!-- --></script>
